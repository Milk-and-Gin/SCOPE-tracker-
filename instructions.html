Understanding the SCOPE Equation

← Back to Tracker

Below is a straightforward explanation of each term in the SCOPE model’s efficacy equation along with suggestions for further reading, including UK/European sources, audiobooks and podcasts.

κ (kappa) – Constant of intention alignment

This constant sets an upper limit on what can be achieved; it reminds us that ritual cannot overturn physical laws. Keep your aim realistic—rituals may shift probabilities, not rewrite reality.

Suggested reading: The Book of English Magic by Philip Carr-Gomm & Richard Heygate. BBC Radio 4’s A History of Magic.

A_focus(t) – Attention and concentration

How intensely you can concentrate on your goal. Neuroscience shows regular exercise improves memory, attention and executive control.

Practical tip: Meditate, do breath-counting, choose a quiet environment.
Reading/listening: The Mind Illuminated (Culadasa), “Clairity with Janey Lee Grace” podcast.

B_belief(t) – Belief and expectancy

Confidence in the method and yourself. Placebo research shows context and expectation create physiological outcomes.

Practical tip: Work within a resonant tradition, build supportive context.
Reading/listening: The Psychology of the Occult (Tony Jinks), “Rune Soup” podcast.

D_attach(t) – Detachment

Ability to let go after the ritual. Over-attachment can choke results.

Practical tip: Distract yourself after ritual, avoid obsession.
Reading/listening: Letting Go (David Hawkins), BBC’s “Mindfulness for Beginners.”

R_res(t) – Resistance and doubt

The absence of inner conflict. Doubt and guilt drain efficacy.

Practical tip: Journal doubts, use CBT, align goals with values.
Reading/listening: The Happiness Trap (Russ Harris), BBC’s “The Life Scientific.”

S_skill(t) – Skill and technique

Proficiency with ritual technologies and practices.

Practical tip: Practise daily, record in a diary.
Reading/listening: Phil Hine’s Condensed Chaos, “The Infinite Monkey Cage” podcast.

L_link(t) – Link or connection

Physical, symbolic, or relational connection between ritual and target.

Practical tip: Personalise talismans, add names, use correspondences.
Reading/listening: Susan Greenwood’s Magic, Witchcraft and the Otherworld, “The Folklore Podcast.”

R_repr(t) – Representation and symbolism

How well symbols express the desired change.

Practical tip: Use clear colours, shapes, archetypes.
Reading/listening: DK’s Signs and Symbols, “Stuff to Blow Your Mind” podcast.

C_coord(t) – Coordination and synchrony

How well actions are synchronised in group or solo rituals.

Practical tip: Practise group rhythms or solo coherence.
Reading/listening: The Art of Ritual (Alexa Kriele), Sacred Drums of Siberia audiobook, BBC’s “Folk Music and Beyond.”

V_vitality(t) – Vitality

Overall health and fitness support magical stamina and clarity.

Practical tip: Exercise, stretch, sleep well, eat balanced.
Reading/listening: John Ratey’s Spark, “The Science of Sport” podcast.

Q(t) – Environmental quality and timing

External context: lunar phases, weather, cultural noise, random chance.

Practical tip: Choose aligned times, clean space, accept noise.
Reading/listening: Nigel Pennick’s The Magical Universe, “Astrology University Podcast.”

Summary

Each factor contributes multiplicatively to efficacy. Train attention, belief, detachment, resolve resistance, practise skills, build links, use strong representations, coordinate actions, maintain vitality, and work in good conditions to maximise outcomes.
